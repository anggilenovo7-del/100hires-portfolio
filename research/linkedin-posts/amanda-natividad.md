## Post 1
**Date:** June 8, 2026
**URL:** [Original Post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amandanat_i-dont-believe-in-launch-days-sure-a-activity-7468684562353729537-JSLc?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADGpi08BcD4FugyYukQKbiz0k7tPk0-E-Ko)

### **Why this post is relevant:**
This post challenges the common belief that a successful launch happens on a single day. Amanda argues that effective product launches are built through a gradual rollout process, starting with the most engaged users and expanding outward over time.

The post highlights a key principle of B2B SaaS content strategy: trust and social proof should be established before broad distribution. Instead of relying on a large announcement, Amanda emphasizes gathering testimonials, real user experiences, and customer validation that can strengthen future marketing efforts.

This approach aligns closely with LinkedIn organic growth, where credibility is often built through customer stories, community engagement, and audience advocacy rather than direct promotion. The post demonstrates how successful marketers use existing audiences to create momentum before reaching a wider market.

Key lessons:
- Product launches should be treated as a phased rollout rather than a single event.
- Early adopters can generate valuable social proof that improves future marketing performance.
- Customer validation is often more persuasive than company-generated promotion.
- Audience trust should be built before scaling distribution to larger audiences.

## Post 2
**Date:** May, 2026
**URL:** [Original Post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amandanat_b2b-buyers-are-on-linkedin-is-true-its-activity-7460350666750955520-BCTa?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADGpi08BcD4FugyYukQKbiz0k7tPk0-E-Ko)

### **Why this post is relevant:**
This post highlights an important principle in B2B SaaS marketing: understanding where an audience spends time is not the same as understanding how they make decisions. Amanda argues that marketers should focus less on channels and more on how ideas spread within an audience.

Using audience research data, she demonstrates that while RevOps leaders are active on LinkedIn, many of their decisions are influenced by internal conversations happening in workplace tools such as Slack. As a result, content should be designed not only to attract attention but also to be shared and discussed among decision-makers.

The post introduces the concept of "forwardable content" — content that people want to bring into team discussions because it helps explain a problem, support a recommendation, or facilitate decision-making. This approach shifts the focus from generating clicks to creating assets that influence buying conversations.

Key lessons:
- Audience research should focus on behavior and decision-making patterns, not just platform usage.
- Content should be designed to support internal discussions among buyers and stakeholders.
- The most effective B2B content is often shared because it helps solve a problem or support a decision.
- Marketing success depends on influencing conversations, not just attracting attention.
