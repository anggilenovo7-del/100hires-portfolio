## Post 1
**Date:** June 11, 2026
**URL:** [Original Post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aprildunford_when-we-have-big-changes-in-a-market-like-activity-7470826617833967616-mXuF?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADGpi08BcD4FugyYukQKbiz0k7tPk0-E-Ko)

### **Why this post is relevant:**

This post highlights the importance of having a clear market point of view, especially during periods of significant industry change such as the rise of AI. April argues that buyers do not only evaluate products — they also pay attention to how companies interpret the future of the market and where they believe the industry is heading.

Using examples from OpenAI, Anthropic, and Microsoft, she demonstrates how different companies communicate distinct perspectives about AI. These perspectives help shape how customers understand each company's role, priorities, and long-term vision. This goes beyond product features and becomes a key part of positioning and brand differentiation.

The post is particularly relevant for B2B SaaS content strategy because it shows how thought leadership can influence market perception. Rather than focusing only on product updates or tactical advice, companies can create stronger positioning by consistently communicating their unique perspective on industry trends and future opportunities.

Key lessons:
- Strong B2B brands communicate a clear point of view about the future of their market.
- Market perspective can be a powerful differentiator when products become increasingly similar.
- Thought leadership content helps shape customer perception before a buying decision is made.
- Buyers often evaluate a company's vision and understanding of the market, not just its product capabilities.

## Post 2
**Date:** May, 2026
**URL:** [Original Post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aprildunford_in-todays-newsletter-im-talking-about-share-7463238628471787521-Lqha/?utm_source=social_share_send&utm_medium=member_desktop_web&rcm=ACoAADGpi08BcD4FugyYukQKbiz0k7tPk0-E-Ko)

### **Why this post is relevant:**

This post provides a practical framework for B2B SaaS positioning during periods of rapid technological change. April explains that as AI transforms software markets, companies must do more than promote product features—they must help customers understand how the market is evolving and how their solutions fit into that future.

The post emphasizes balancing long-term vision with present-day customer needs. While many companies are eager to position themselves around future AI capabilities, April argues that effective positioning should remain grounded in the problems customers are facing today. This helps avoid creating messaging that feels disconnected from current buyer priorities.

The post also reinforces the importance of customer research. During times of market disruption, assumptions can become dangerous. Companies that maintain close contact with customer needs, concerns, and buying behavior are better equipped to create relevant messaging and make effective positioning decisions.

Key lessons:
- B2B SaaS companies need a clear point of view on how AI is changing their market.
- Positioning should address current customer challenges while supporting a long-term vision.
- Messaging that focuses too heavily on future possibilities can become disconnected from customer reality.
- Continuous customer research is critical during periods of rapid market change.

## Post 3
**Date:** February, 2026
**URL:** [Original Post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aprildunford_you-dont-want-to-start-any-positioning-work-activity-7422666982032056322-QbQr?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADGpi08BcD4FugyYukQKbiz0k7tPk0-E-Ko)

### **Why this post is relevant:**

This post demonstrates that effective positioning begins with strategic clarity rather than messaging. April argues that many companies rush into positioning exercises before defining critical fundamentals, such as the target audience, the product being positioned, and the specific buyer persona whose needs the positioning should address.

The post highlights a common challenge in B2B SaaS organizations: different teams often have different assumptions about the purpose of positioning. Without alignment, positioning efforts can become inefficient, leading to conflicting messaging and unnecessary rework. By defining key decisions upfront, companies can create positioning that is more consistent, actionable, and aligned with business objectives.

This perspective is highly relevant to B2B SaaS content strategy because strong content depends on clear positioning. Marketers cannot effectively communicate value if they do not first understand who they are targeting, what they are positioning, and how customers evaluate solutions.

Key lessons:
- Positioning should begin with strategic decisions, not messaging creation.
- Organizations must align on the purpose and audience of positioning before starting the process.
- Customer understanding is a prerequisite for effective positioning.
- Clear positioning creates a stronger foundation for marketing, sales, and product communication.
