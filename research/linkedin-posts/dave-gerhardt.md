## Post 1
**Date:** June 10, 2026
**URL:** [Original Post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_we-polled-540-b2b-marketers-on-how-they-feel-activity-7470420490621464576-MWC_?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADGpi08BcD4FugyYukQKbiz0k7tPk0-E-Ko)

### **Why this post is relevant:**

This post explores how B2B marketers are adapting to AI and highlights the growing importance of human judgment in an AI-driven environment. Using survey data from hundreds of marketers, Dave identifies a key trend: most professionals are not afraid of AI replacing their jobs, but they are concerned about the increasing volume of low-quality content being produced with AI tools.

The post is relevant because it shifts the conversation from automation to differentiation. Rather than focusing on AI as a threat, Dave argues that the real competitive advantage will come from creativity, taste, strategic thinking, and the ability to produce high-quality work. This perspective aligns with the broader trend in B2B marketing where audience trust and content quality are becoming more important as AI-generated content becomes more common.

The post also demonstrates how original audience research can be used to create thought leadership content. By collecting and sharing insights from his own community, Dave creates content that is both informative and highly relevant to his target audience.

Key lessons:
- AI adoption is becoming standard among B2B marketers.
- The biggest concern is content quality degradation rather than job displacement.
- Human judgment, creativity, and strategic thinking remain valuable differentiators.
- Original research can be an effective source of thought leadership content.

## Post 2
**Date:** June, 2026
**URL:** [Original Post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_i-love-writing-about-abm-and-our-audience-activity-7468617621387636736-Z35m?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADGpi08BcD4FugyYukQKbiz0k7tPk0-E-Ko)

### **Why this post is relevant:**

This post demonstrates how effective B2B SaaS content can transform expert interviews into actionable insights for a broader audience. Dave summarizes key lessons from an ABM (Account-Based Marketing) practitioner and presents them in a practical, easy-to-consume format that helps marketers understand how successful ABM programs operate in real companies.

A key theme throughout the post is alignment between sales and marketing. Rather than focusing on attribution or internal credit, Dave highlights how ABM succeeds when both teams work toward a shared objective. This reflects a broader shift in B2B marketing from channel-centric thinking toward revenue-focused collaboration.

The post also combines tactical advice with forward-looking perspectives. While readers learn practical ABM strategies that can be implemented today, they are also introduced to emerging concepts such as AI-powered personalization and agent-driven ABM. This balance between immediate value and future trends is a common characteristic of effective thought leadership content.

Key lessons:
- Successful ABM programs are built around sales capacity and account prioritization.
- Marketing and sales alignment is often more important than attribution reporting.
- High-impact ABM campaigns focus resources on a small number of strategically important accounts.
- AI may significantly improve personalization and account engagement in future ABM programs.

## Post 3
**Date:** June, 2026
**URL:** [Original Post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_im-worried-about-my-brain-am-i-relying-activity-7468284788337065984-bqiJ?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADGpi08BcD4FugyYukQKbiz0k7tPk0-E-Ko)

### **Why this post is relevant:**

This post explores the unintended consequences of relying too heavily on AI and instant access to information. Dave reflects on how tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity make it possible to answer almost any question immediately, while raising concerns about whether this convenience reduces opportunities for deep thinking, reflection, and creativity.

Rather than rejecting AI, Dave advocates for balancing technological efficiency with intentional habits that encourage independent thinking. He uses journaling as an example of a practice that slows down the thinking process, improves idea generation, and creates space for reflection in an increasingly AI-driven world.

The post is relevant because it addresses a growing challenge for knowledge workers and marketers: how to use AI as a tool without becoming overly dependent on it. It also demonstrates how personal experiences can be used to discuss broader industry trends in a relatable and engaging way.

Key lessons:
- AI can improve access to information but may reduce opportunities for deep reflection if used passively.
- Writing and journaling can strengthen creativity, idea generation, and critical thinking.
- Personal stories are effective for discussing broader technology and workplace trends.
- The goal is not to avoid AI, but to use it in ways that enhance rather than replace human thinking.
