## Post 3
**Date:** June 13, 2026
**URL:** [Original Post](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/two-speed-problem-justin-welsh-wr6ne)

### **Why this post is relevant:**

This post illustrates an important principle in B2B SaaS content strategy: successful creators optimize for business outcomes rather than audience opinions. Justin shares a real example of receiving negative feedback after redesigning his website, yet achieving significantly better conversion results because the design was aligned with a specific business goal.

The post demonstrates how experienced creators and entrepreneurs use data-driven decision making instead of reacting to immediate public feedback. This mindset is highly relevant to LinkedIn organic growth, where content performance should be measured by outcomes such as audience growth, engagement quality, lead generation, or newsletter subscriptions rather than likes or opinions alone.

The post also showcases a common content pattern used by top LinkedIn creators: combining a personal story with a practical business lesson. This makes complex marketing concepts more relatable and memorable while reinforcing the author's credibility.

Key lessons:
- Define clear business objectives before creating content or making strategic changes.
- Measure success using data and outcomes rather than audience reactions alone.
- Not all feedback is equally valuable; prioritize feedback aligned with your goals.
- Personal stories can effectively communicate strategic marketing principles.
