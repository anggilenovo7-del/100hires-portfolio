## Post 1
**Date:** June 8, 2026
**URL:** [Original Post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/peeplaja_most-agency-founders-or-consultants-are-thinking-activity-7469704785223426049-ReYL?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADGpi08BcD4FugyYukQKbiz0k7tPk0-E-Ko)

### **Why this post is relevant:**

This post challenges a common aspiration among agency owners: transitioning from a service business to a scalable product business. Rather than focusing on success stories alone, Peep outlines the operational, financial, and leadership requirements that make such a transition possible.

The post emphasizes that building a new business requires dedicated resources, leadership, and long-term commitment. Peep argues that many agency owners fail because they attempt to build a product company as a side project while maintaining their existing business. Instead, successful transitions require a founder's direct involvement, a separate team, and sufficient funding to support the new venture through its early stages.

The post is relevant because it highlights an important principle in growth and business strategy: meaningful transformation requires focus. Companies often underestimate the resources, time, and organizational changes needed to pursue new opportunities. By sharing practical lessons from his own experience, Peep provides a realistic framework for evaluating business expansion and innovation efforts.

Key lessons:
- New ventures require dedicated leadership and cannot be treated as side projects.
- Successful business transitions depend on resource allocation, not just strategy.
- Founders play a critical role during the early stages of building new products.
- Long-term funding and patience are often necessary before achieving profitability.

## Post 2
**Date:** June 7, 2026
**URL:** [Original Post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/peeplaja_whats-it-like-running-3-companies-at-once-activity-7468255259719647233-EdDj?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADGpi08BcD4FugyYukQKbiz0k7tPk0-E-Ko)

### **Why this post is relevant:**

This post highlights an important principle of business growth: sustainable companies are built around systems and leaders, not individual founders. Peep reflects on his experience transitioning away from operational roles in multiple companies and the fear that those businesses would struggle without his direct involvement.

The post demonstrates that long-term scalability depends on developing strong internal leaders who can take ownership and drive the business forward. Rather than remaining at the center of every decision, founders must create an environment where others can succeed independently. This allows the organization to continue growing while enabling founders to pursue new opportunities.

The post is particularly relevant because it challenges a common assumption among entrepreneurs and business leaders—that they are indispensable. Peep argues that a founder's role is to accelerate growth and create momentum, but not to become the sole driver of the company's success.

Key lessons:
- Sustainable businesses should not depend entirely on the founder.
- Developing internal leadership is critical for long-term growth.
- Delegation and trust are essential components of organizational scaling.
- Founders create more value when they build systems and leaders rather than controlling every function.

## Post 3
**Date:** June 1, 2026
**URL:** [Original Post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/peeplaja_five-things-b2b-companies-getting-ai-roi-activity-7466443296350023680-Pnry?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADGpi08BcD4FugyYukQKbiz0k7tPk0-E-Ko)

### **Why this post is relevant:**

This post explores why some B2B companies achieve measurable ROI from AI while many others struggle to see meaningful results. Drawing on insights from marketing leaders, Peep argues that successful AI adoption is not primarily a technology challenge but a strategy and execution challenge.

The post highlights a recurring theme in modern B2B marketing: AI amplifies existing strengths and weaknesses. Organizations with clear positioning, strong customer understanding, and disciplined operational processes tend to generate better outcomes because they use AI to enhance an already effective strategy. In contrast, companies that treat AI as a standalone solution often fail to achieve significant results.

The post is also valuable because it focuses on practical implementation rather than AI hype. The recommendations emphasize process improvement, workflow automation, resource allocation, and strategic clarity, making the lessons applicable to a wide range of B2B organizations.

Key lessons:
- AI is most effective when built on strong strategic fundamentals.
- Organizations should view AI as a force multiplier rather than a cost-cutting tool.
- Disciplined technology adoption often produces better ROI than rapid experimentation.
- AI can create significant value by addressing operational bottlenecks and manual workflows.
