## Post 1
**Date:** June 2, 2026
**URL:** [Original Post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/randfishkin_the-truth-is-tech-doesnt-have-an-image-share-7466980273725800448-Pj0u/?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADGpi08BcD4FugyYukQKbiz0k7tPk0-E-Ko)

### **Why this post is relevant:**

This post argues that public criticism of technology companies is often rooted in concerns about incentives and consequences rather than communication failures. Rand highlights the idea that the problem is not how technology companies explain their actions, but the actions and objectives themselves.

The post is relevant because it challenges a common assumption in marketing and public relations: that perception problems can always be solved through better messaging. Instead, it emphasizes the importance of aligning business decisions with customer, societal, and ethical considerations. According to this perspective, strong communication cannot compensate for products or strategies that people fundamentally view as harmful.

The post also demonstrates how thought leaders can engage audiences by addressing broader industry debates rather than focusing solely on tactical marketing topics. By discussing incentives, ethics, and long-term consequences, Rand expands the conversation beyond product features and business growth.

Key lessons:
- Messaging cannot solve problems caused by misaligned incentives or harmful outcomes.
- Public perception is often shaped by actions more than communication strategies.
- Thought leadership can be strengthened by addressing industry-wide issues and ethical concerns.
- Businesses should consider the broader impact of their decisions, not just financial outcomes.

The post is particularly relevant because it highlights a common problem in marketing and technology industries: trend-driven decision making. As new technologies emerge, marketers and business leaders often react to headlines, social media discussions, and anecdotal evidence. Rand encourages a more disciplined approach by examining real-world data before making strategic decisions.

The content also reinforces the value of independent research and market intelligence. By questioning assumptions and validating claims with data, marketers can avoid overreacting to hype cycles and focus on opportunities that reflect actual customer behavior.

Key lessons:
- Marketing decisions should be guided by data rather than industry hype.
- Popular narratives are not always supported by real-world evidence.
- Emerging technologies should be evaluated based on actual adoption and usage patterns.
- Independent research can provide a competitive advantage by revealing what is truly happening in the market.

## Post 3
**Date:** May, 2026
**URL:** [Original Post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/randfishkin_heres-a-great-zero-click-marketing-case-ugcPost-7460787899236515840-ks4i/?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADGpi08BcD4FugyYukQKbiz0k7tPk0-E-Ko)

### **Why this post is relevant:**

This post demonstrates how businesses can grow by becoming highly visible within the places where their audience already spends time, rather than relying solely on website traffic or direct-response marketing. Rand uses Mark Powell Bespoke as a case study to illustrate the principles of Zero-Click Marketing, where awareness, trust, and demand are built through content consumption without requiring immediate clicks or conversions.

The post highlights that successful marketing often comes from aligning a strong product with the right audience, distribution channel, and industry influencers. Instead of spreading efforts across multiple platforms, Mark Powell invested deeply in Instagram, continuously improving content quality and building visibility within a niche community. This focused approach allowed him to become a recognized figure in the classic menswear space.

The post also reinforces the importance of earned attention. By appearing consistently across influential media sources, creators, and communities, businesses can increase awareness and trust without relying exclusively on paid advertising or search traffic.

Key lessons:
- Strong audience-channel alignment is often more important than being present everywhere.
- Zero-Click Marketing focuses on building awareness where audiences already spend time.
- Consistent content improvement compounds over time.
- PR, influencer relationships, and industry visibility can significantly amplify business growth.
