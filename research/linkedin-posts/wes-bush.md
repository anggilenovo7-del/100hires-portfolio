### **Why this post is relevant:**

This post demonstrates the importance of understanding how customers actually make purchasing decisions rather than relying on assumptions or incomplete attribution models. Wes explains how AI can automate customer journey analysis by combining data from multiple touchpoints and generating a complete narrative of what influenced a customer's decision to buy.

The post is relevant because many B2B companies struggle to understand which marketing and sales activities truly contribute to customer acquisition. Traditional attribution models often focus on a single touchpoint, while real buying journeys involve multiple interactions across channels. By automatically connecting these interactions, teams can gain a more accurate understanding of how customers discover, evaluate, and purchase products.

The post also highlights a practical application of AI in business operations. Rather than using AI to create content, Wes uses it to improve decision-making by uncovering patterns in customer behavior. This enables teams to identify successful acquisition strategies and allocate resources more effectively.

Key lessons:
- Customer journeys are often more complex than traditional attribution models suggest.
- AI can help automate customer research and buying journey analysis.
- Understanding how customers buy leads to better marketing and growth decisions.
- Data-driven insights are often more valuable than assumptions about customer behavior.

## Post 2
**Date:** June 2, 2026
**URL:** [Original Post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/wesbush_if-a-10m-sales-led-ceo-asked-me-to-help-activity-7466145861295833088-pk6d?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADGpi08BcD4FugyYukQKbiz0k7tPk0-E-Ko)

### **Why this post is relevant:**

This post explains why successful Product-Led Growth (PLG) adoption requires organizational alignment rather than tactical changes alone. Wes argues that many companies fail with PLG because they focus on execution details such as pricing or free trials before establishing the strategic foundations needed to support a product-led approach.

The post emphasizes that PLG is not simply a growth tactic but a company-wide operating model. Leadership commitment, product capabilities, user experience, and organizational priorities must all support the goal of helping users experience value before making a purchase decision. Without these elements, companies often struggle to achieve meaningful results regardless of how their free trial or onboarding experience is designed.

The post is particularly relevant because it highlights the relationship between customer experience and revenue growth. Rather than relying primarily on sales teams to explain value, PLG companies design products that allow users to discover value directly through product usage. This creates stronger customer engagement and improves conversion opportunities.

Key lessons:
- Product-Led Growth requires organizational commitment, not just tactical changes.
- Leadership alignment is a critical prerequisite for successful PLG implementation.
- User experience and time-to-value are core drivers of product-led success.
- Customers who experience value before speaking with sales are easier to convert.

## Post 3
**Date:** June 1, 2026
**URL:** [Original Post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/wesbush_had-a-blast-speaking-at-saastanak-on-how-activity-7465026524518273024-8xJq?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADGpi08BcD4FugyYukQKbiz0k7tPk0-E-Ko)

### **Why this post is relevant:**

This post explains how Product-Led Growth is evolving in the AI era. Wes argues that companies can no longer rely solely on feature sets or product complexity as competitive advantages. Instead, successful products must help users achieve meaningful outcomes quickly while minimizing effort and friction.

The framework focuses on three critical elements: designing an outcome-focused product experience, reducing time-to-value, and creating differentiated solutions through strong product opinions. By leveraging AI to automate complex tasks, companies can make sophisticated products accessible to a wider audience while improving user activation and engagement.

The post is particularly relevant because it highlights a major shift in software adoption. As AI lowers the barriers to building products, long-term differentiation increasingly comes from how effectively products solve problems and how quickly users experience success. This perspective aligns product strategy directly with customer value and business growth.

Key lessons:
- Users care more about outcomes than product features or processes.
- Time-to-value is becoming a critical competitive advantage.
- AI can reduce friction and expand product accessibility.
- Differentiation increasingly comes from unique approaches to solving customer problems rather than technology alone.
