## Ultimate Guide to Social Media Marketing for B2B in 2026
**URL:** [Youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lOBVe0M2DaE)
**Published:** April 27, 2026

### Transcript / Key Points
**1. Most B2B Companies Use Social Media Wrong**
The biggest mistake is treating social media like another advertising channel.

Most companies post:
- Product updates
- Webinar promotions
- Blog links
- Corporate announcements

But people don't open Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, or YouTube because they want ads.

They open those platforms because they want to:
- Laugh
- Learn
- Feel something
- Be entertained

Chris argues that product-centric content is the reason most B2B social strategies fail.

**2. Every Piece of Content Should Do One of Three Things**
Before publishing anything, ClickUp asks:
- Does it make people feel something?
- Does it teach something useful?
- Does it provide value?

If content doesn't satisfy at least one of those goals, they don't publish it.

**3. Don't Create for Every Platform**
A common misconception is that companies need different content strategies for every social network.

Chris recommends:
- Create for only 1–2 primary platforms
- Repurpose across the rest

At ClickUp:
**Instagram/TikTok**
- Business humor
- Entertainment-first content

**LinkedIn**
- Thought leadership
- Industry insights

Everything else gets adapted from those core content streams.

**4. A/B Test Social Content Like Paid Ads**
One of ClickUp's biggest advantages is using Instagram Trial Reels.

For every video they create:
- Multiple hooks
- Multiple headlines
- Multiple edits

Instead of guessing which version works, they test them.
Chris notes that the version creators think will win is often not the version audiences actually prefer.

**5. Deep ICP Knowledge Is the Real Moat**
ClickUp's content engine starts with understanding customers.

Chris regularly schedules short interviews with:
- Customers
- Prospects
- ICPs

Questions include:
- What podcasts do you listen to?
- What content do you consume?
- What made you laugh recently?
- What frustrations do you deal with at work?

Those conversations become the raw material for future content.

**6. The Best Content Ideas Come From Real Conversations**
Rather than brainstorming in isolation, ClickUp mines content directly from customer conversations.

This creates:
- More authentic content
- Better jokes
- More relatable situations
- Higher engagement

The closer marketers stay to their audience, the easier content creation becomes.

**7. Social Media Can Drive Enterprise Pipeline**
One of the most interesting parts of the conversation is how social content eventually translated into revenue.

Examples include:
- Enterprise buyers mentioning videos during sales cycles
- Candidates discovering ClickUp through social content
- Major accounts citing social media as their first touchpoint
- Large customers such as VaynerMedia becoming aware of ClickUp through content exposure

Attribution isn't always direct, but social increasingly influences purchasing decisions.

**8. ClickUp Runs a Weekly "Writer's Room"**
Their production process resembles a TV show.

**Monday**
- Everyone pitches ideas
- Team votes

**Tuesday**
- Refine scripts

**Wednesday**
- Final reviews

**Thursday**
- Shoot 12–15 videos

**Friday**
- Analyze performance

Then the cycle repeats.

**9. Volume Matters, But Only Up To A Point**
Initially, ClickUp increased output aggressively.

Results:
- 30M monthly impressions
- Then 100M+
- Eventually 200M+

However, they discovered that posting more wasn't always better.

**For some channels:**
- One great video per day outperformed multiple posts

**The lesson:**
More content helps, but audience fatigue is real.

**10. You Don't Need a Huge Budget**
ClickUp's early setup was surprisingly simple:
- One creator
- One marketer
- iPhones
- Basic editing

No fancy production.

**The focus was on:**
- Good ideas
- Consistency
- udience understanding

Production quality came later.

**11. Find Small Creators Before Everyone Else Does**
Chris recommends looking for creators who have:
- 3k–10k followers
- A few viral posts
- Strong content instincts

**These creators are often:**
- Affordable
- Hungry
- Easier to recruit

Rather than hiring agencies, ClickUp directly recruits emerging talent from social platforms.

**12. AI Is Useful, But Human Creativity Wins**
Chris uses AI tools and experiments with AI-generated content.

**However, his belief is simple:**
- People still prefer content made by humans.

As AI-generated content becomes more common, authentic human content becomes more valuable.

**People are increasingly drawn to:**
- Real personalities
- Real stories
- Real humor
- Real experiences

Rather than replacing creators, AI may actually increase the value of genuine human creativity.

**13. Social Media Is Becoming a Competitive Moat**
Chris argues that product differentiation alone is becoming harder.
AI is making software easier to build.

That means:
- More competitors
- Faster feature parity
- Lower barriers to entry

As a result, brand becomes increasingly important.

**Social media creates:**
- Familiarity
- Trust
- Preference
- Mindshare

Long before buyers enter a sales process.

**Why this video is relevant:**
This conversation is one of the most practical B2B social media playbooks available today.

Instead of treating social as a distribution channel, ClickUp treats it as:
- Brand building
- Audience research
- Creator marketing
- Demand generation

The most important insight is that successful B2B social content often looks nothing like traditional B2B marketing.

Rather than constantly talking about the product, the best brands:
- Entertain
- Educate
- Build relationships
- Create memorable experiences

As AI makes products increasingly similar, attention and trust may become the strongest competitive advantages.
For founders, marketers, and GTM leaders, this interview shows how content can evolve from a marketing tactic into a long-term growth moat.
